Moving the Value: Transportation in the Phosphate Industry

Once prepared, finished fertilizers and acid are loaded for transport to export terminals or distribution centers. Within the plant complex, conveyors and elevators move product from storage to load-out points (at Jorf Lasfar there are over 100 conveyors linking production lines to the docks). From there:

Trucks:

Bulk fertilizer is hauled by covered bulk trucks or bagged product by flatbed semi-trailers with tarps. Phosphoric acid is transported in dedicated stainless-steel or HDPE-lined tank trucks. All vehicles meet ADR (UN) regulations for corrosive/hazardous goods: trucks carry spill kits, grounding cables, and drivers are specially trained. Loaders use dust-free hoppers and secured fittings.

Rail:

Heavy cargoes (especially to domestic distribution centers) use rail. Covered hopper wagons carry granulated fertilizers (to keep them dry), while pressure-rated tank cars carry acid. Morocco’s ONCF railway has direct spurs from plants (e.g. Benguerir → Safi, Khouribga → Jorf Lasfar). Rail transport allows large batch shipments with strict scheduling.

Conveyors:

On-site belt conveyors or pipe conveyors (e.g. Morocco’s 102 km slurry pipeline for ore) also move products. At Jorf Lasfar the fertilizer lines tie directly into quay conveyors for ship loading. Conveyors are fitted with safety pull-cords, slip/stop sensors and covered belts to contain dust.


All transport operations follow strict safety and efficiency protocols. Vehicles are weighed on automatic weighbridges. Routing is optimized (with GPS tracking) to reduce distance and emissions. Loading/unloading areas have ventilation and spill containment. Digital systems track each shipment’s origin, batch and destination to ensure traceability. During loading, personnel monitor for leaks or dust. Spill response teams stand by at distribution points, and emergency exercises are regularly conducted. Efficiency is also achieved by consolidating loads – for example, grouping fertilizer grades by port destination – and by coordinating train and ship schedules to minimize idle time and demurrage.
