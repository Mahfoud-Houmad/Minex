Gateway to the World: Port Operations for Phosphate Exports

Exports of phosphate products rely on Morocco’s modern port terminals. The primary export port for fertilizers is Jorf Lasfar, which sits on the Atlantic coast. This terminal has large storage yards (roofed silos and blending domes) near six berths. Finished fertilizers are fed to the berths via reclaim conveyors. Specialized shiploaders – telescoping belt conveyors – can load two vessels simultaneously at about 2,000 tonnes/hour each. Sulfur and other bulk inputs have separate loaders. The port area covers ~5 km² and includes dust-collection units and conveyor covers. For phosphoric acid (and sulphuric acid), pipelines and pumps connect storage tanks directly to chemical tanker berths. Loading operations use enclosures or water curtains to suppress airborne dust. Fire-fighting monitors and spill-retention pools line the quay.

Jorf Lasfar (El Jadida):

Morocco’s largest fertilizer export terminal. It handles all grades of MAP/DAP/NPK. Equipped with dual shiploaders (2×2000 t/h) and conveyor bridges. Contained domes store bulk granules. Ships up to Panamax size (70–80k dwt) are routinely loaded. Environmental systems (fog cannons, sealed conveyors) minimize pollution during loading.

Safi:

A second OCP coastal facility, handling phosphate rock and fertilizer. Safi has its own reactors (acid plant) and docks for bulkers. An on-site rail link brings rock from Gantour mines to Safi’s washery and fertilizer plant, then conveyors to port wharves. Continuous ambient-air monitors and water-treatment units at Safi help control emissions.

Casablanca:

Historically a phosphate export port (Somaport terminal), it still ships mineral fertilizers and rock. A rail spur links Khouribga mine to Casablanca. On-deck conveyors and shiploaders (capacity ~1,500–2,000 t/h) handle the bulk loads. Dust filters and port drainage systems mitigate contamination.

Environmental Protections:

All OCP port terminals employ best practices. Dust is controlled by water sprays and cyclones; stormwater is routed through neutralization ponds. Storage areas have impervious floors and collection drains. OCP has implemented real-time dust monitoring and invested in “green port” technologies. Ships comply with MARPOL rules (e.g. no ballast water discharge). Solid waste and washdown water are treated or recycled. Regular audits (internal and regulatory) ensure adherence to environmental standards.


The final-product stage of the phosphate chain combines engineered storage and transport systems with rigorous safety and sustainability measures. In Morocco, OCP’s integrated approach – from climate-controlled fertilizer halls and lined acid tanks to high-speed ship loaders and green port policies – ensures that billions of tonnes of phosphate products are delivered worldwide reliably and responsibly.
