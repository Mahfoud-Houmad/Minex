Keeping the Pit Safe: Geotechnical Monitoring and Slope Stability

Continuous monitoring of pit walls and tailings ensures early detection of instability and supports safe operations. Instrumentation—piezometers, extensometers, slope radar and survey prisms—tracks displacement, pore pressures and acceleration. Data integrate to slope stability models and trigger thresholds (traffic restriction, dewatering increase, bench re-design). Regular geotechnical inspections complement instrument monitoring; recorded trends inform long-term pit sequencing and rehabilitation design.

Environmental & Social Management

ESMS guides how the project minimises environmental harm and engages communities — covering baseline studies, monitoring, grievance mechanisms and benefit sharing. An Environmental and Social Management System includes an Environmental Management Plan (EMP), monitoring programs, community engagement plans, grievance mechanisms and livelihood restoration where required. It sets performance indicators, reporting frequency, roles and responsibilities and links to corporate sustainability objectives. Transparent disclosure and local hiring programs strengthen the social licence to operate.

EMP / Baseline & Monitoring

The EMP documents impact mitigation, monitoring schedules and legal compliance responsibilities for the mine. EMP elements include water and air quality monitoring, biodiversity management, waste handling, noise control and monitoring frequencies. Baseline datasets define pre-impact conditions against which operational impacts are measured. The EMP specifies corrective actions, reporting lines and adaptive management measures where monitoring shows deviations from expected performance.

Community Engagement & Grievance Mechanisms

Sustained two-way engagement builds trust; grievance systems provide a transparent way for concerns to be raised and resolved. Engagement plans include regular consultations, local hiring targets, community development programs and transparent grievance channels. Grievances are logged, investigated within set timelines and outcomes communicated. Social impact monitoring tracks indicators such as employment, income changes and health outcomes. Capacity building (training, supplier development) helps spread economic benefits beyond the mine
