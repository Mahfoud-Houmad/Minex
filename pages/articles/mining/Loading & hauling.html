Moving the Rock: Loading and Hauling Operations in Open-Pit Mines

Effective loading and hauling requires matching excavator/shovel bucket size to truck payload, optimising cycle times and payload utilisation. Road geometry, grades and passing bays minimise queuing; fleet telematics provide utilisation, idle time and fuel consumption metrics. Operational techniques (push loading, spot loading) and tyre/fuel management reduce unit costs. A structured maintenance program preserves availability and predictable productivity in harsh pit conditions.

Shovel-Truck Matching & Fleet Sizing

Choose equipment combinations that hit production targets with minimal idle time and fuel penalty. Designers size fleets by calculating required tonnes/hour, truck cycle time and loader fill time; excavator bucket must match truck payload for efficient single-pass loading. Fleet simulations and load-haul analysis determine number of trucks, spare ratios and shift coverage. Modern operations use telematics to refine matching over time and to plan equipment purchases or redeployment.

Loading Techniques & Cycle Optimisation

Loading method influences cycle time, tyre life and fragmentation needs—optimisation improves productivity across the pit. Techniques include spot loading, push loading and pre-charging, chosen based on layout, fragmentation and truck type. Operators are trained for consistent bucket fill, reducing multiple passes and tyre wear. Cycle time is minimised by optimising queue management, staging areas and by ensuring predictable shovel availability; telemetry metrics drive continuous improvement.

Internal Roads & Route Design

Road maintenance and design control travel time, safety and operating costs for haulage fleets. Roads are engineered with appropriate crossfall, drainage and compaction to support truck loads and to minimise erosion. Regular grading and maintenance schedules, controlled speeds and designated passing bays reduce incidents and rolling resistance. Use of stabilising binders in high-traffic areas extends maintenance intervals; wheelwash stations at site exits prevent off-site sediment transfer.

Fleet Management & Telematics

Telematics provide the data needed to manage productivity, fuel use and maintenance planning for hundreds of mobile assets. Fleet management systems track location, engine hours, fuel burn, idle time and load cycles in real time, enabling dispatch optimisation and predictive maintenance. Analytics identify bottlenecks (waiting at shovel, queuing), support tyre management programs and allow KPI dashboards for supervisors. Integration with pit planning software aligns haulage flows to short-term production schedules.

Maintenance & Safety Procedures

Routine maintenance and safety inspections keep heavy equipment available and reduce risks on site. Preventive maintenance schedules, daily pre-start checks, tyre pressure monitoring and condition-based alerts reduce breakdowns and catastrophic failures. Lock-out/tag-out, isolation procedures and operator training on slope travel and braking are core safety elements. Incident reporting, root cause analysis and continuous training underpin a mature safety culture.
