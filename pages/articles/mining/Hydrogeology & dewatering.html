Managing Water Underground: Hydrogeology and Dewatering in Mines

Dewatering plans manage groundwater inflows to pits while protecting nearby aquifers and water users. Dewatering wells and pumps lower water tables locally; cut-off drains and grouting can limit lateral inflows. Pumped groundwater is treated if required for pH, turbidity or dissolved solids before reuse or discharge. Monitoring networks track groundwater draw-down, recovery and potential impacts on adjacent wells. Dewatering design is integrated with pit slope and geotechnical stability to avoid destabilisation.
