Breaking Ground: The Process of Mine Excavation in Phosphate Mining”

Excavator on groundwork on dramatic twilight. Excavator on sunset background.

Excavation involves the systematic removal of overburden (non-ore material) and phosphate ore using a combination of drilling, blasting, and mechanical loading. The process is carefully planned to optimise ore recovery, maintain slope stability, and minimise environmental impact. Detailed mine plans dictate the sequence of excavation, bench heights, and pit geometries, while geotechnical monitoring ensures slope safety. Excavation also includes stockpiling of different material types (overburden, low-grade ore) for future use or rehabilitation.

Overburden Removal & Stockpiling

Overburden (soil and rock above the phosphate seam) is removed in controlled layers and temporarily stored for site rehabilitation or backfilling. Excavation follows a bench-by-bench approach to maintain stability. Stockpiles are placed on stable, low-erosion areas, contoured to reduce runoff and seeded if storage is long-term. GPS and volume tracking ensures accurate placement and future reuse. Measures prevent contamination from fuels or oils, preserving soil for eventual reclamation.

Ore Excavation & Handling

Phosphate ore is extracted using hydraulic excavators, loaders, and haul trucks. Ore is selectively mined to maintain grade control, separating high-grade material from lower-grade or waste rock. Loading sequences minimise truck travel and fuel consumption. Ore stockpiles are constructed with controlled compaction and drainage to prevent erosion or waterlogging. Sampling and quality testing occur continuously to ensure compliance with processing specifications.

Slope & Safety Management

Pit walls are designed to stable angles based on rock type, depth, and geotechnical assessments. Benches are maintained with proper width to allow machinery access. Safety berms and catch benches prevent accidental rockfalls. Monitoring equipment (inclinometers, piezometers) tracks slope movement and groundwater pressures. Emergency evacuation routes and clear signage are maintained throughout the excavation area.

Water & Environmental Management

Excavation activities are integrated with water and sediment control measures to protect downstream ecosystems. Runoff from benches and stockpiles is directed to sedimentation ponds or channels. Oil/water separators are installed for workshops and refuelling areas. Dust suppression (water sprays, windbreaks) mitigates particulate emissions. Progressive rehabilitation involves backfilling low-grade overburden and regrading disturbed areas.
