Controlling the Path: Dust and Road Management for Sustainable Mining

Mine dewatering helps manage water levels so mining operations can run smoothly and say on schedule, following what’s been planned by the engineering plan.

A layered dust control programme couples source controls (enclosures, sprays), roadway treatments (water trucks, hygroscopic binders) and operational measures (speed limits, wheel washes). Continuous PM monitoring (PM10/PM2.5) at critical receptors and meteorological stations allows dynamic responses (increase suppression during high winds). Records of interventions and community complaints feed continuous improvement and compliance reporting.

Dust Suppression Techniques (sprinklers, misting, binders)

Use water, misting, chemical binders and localized capture to prevent dust liberation at source. Sprinklers and misting cannons suppress dust at crushers, stockpiles and transfer points. Road binders (calcium chloride, lignosulfonates) stabilise surfaces to reduce resuspension; surfactants improve water efficiency. Enclosures and local ventilation capture dust for extraction and filtration where feasible. Choice of technique balances effectiveness, water availability and environmental impacts of binders

Road Maintenance & Stabilisation

Well-maintained internal roads reduce dust generation and operating costs. Regular grading, application of stabilising material and planned watering schedules keep surfaces firm. High-use routes may be paved or chemically stabilised. Drainage and surfacing prevent rutting and ponding that break surface cohesion. Road maintenance KPIs include % roads graded per week and litres of water applied per km.

Air Quality Monitoring (PM10/PM2.5)

Continuous or periodic measurement of particulate matter ensures compliance and helps trigger suppression actions. Fixed monitoring stations record PM10 and PM2.5 concentrations at strategic points; portable monitors are used for spot checks. Data dashboards show trends and trigger alarms if thresholds are exceeded. Correlating PM spikes with operations (blasting, high winds, crushing) allows targeted mitigation. Public dashboards and complaint logs improve transparency with local stakeholders.
