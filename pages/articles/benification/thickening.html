Concentrating the Slurry: Thickening in Mineral Processing

Thickening is the process of increasing the solid content of the slurry that comes out of flotation.

The slurry contains water, phosphate concentrate, and fine waste particles. By allowing the solids to settle and separating most of the water, thickening produces a denser material that is easier to handle and transport

The principle behind thickening relies on gravity settling, sometimes aided by chemical flocculants that cause fine particles to clump together and settle faster. Recovered water is usually sent back into the process, reducing freshwater needs.

Thickening is important not just for efficiency, but also for environmental compliance — it reduces the volume of waste slurry and prevents unnecessary water loss.
