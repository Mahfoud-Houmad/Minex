Morocco

Over 50 billion tonnes of phosphate rock spread across key sites fueling food security worldwide

Positioned at the crossroads of Africa and Europe, Morocco holds over 70% of the world’s phosphate reserves, making it the undisputed leader in this vital mineral resource. From the vast mining sites of Khouribga to the rich deposits in Bou Craa, Morocco’s phosphate industry fuels global agriculture and industry.

Morocco’s Phosphate Reserves

Khouribga, Morocco’s largest phosphate reserve, is the beating heart of global phosphate supply.

The Khouribga mine, discovered in 1917, is the world’s largest phosphate deposit. Its strategic location and massive reserves make Morocco the global leader in phosphate production. The region plays a crucial role in both domestic development and international agriculture.
